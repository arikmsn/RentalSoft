# System Specification Document - Equipment Tracking & Field Operations (V1)

## 1. Introduction

### 1.1 Background and Vision

This system is designed for companies that install and temporarily leave equipment at customer sites for short periods (typically one to two weeks), similar to a rental or temporary deployment model. The main pain points today include a lack of precise knowledge about each device's location, missed removal dates, equipment loss in the field, and the absence of a clear history of actions performed at each site and what is required next [1].

The **vision of the system** is to become the "brain of the equipment in the field" by:

*   Always knowing where each tagged device is, at which customer site, and for how long.
*   Displaying the history of actions performed during the last installation/inspection/removal and what needs to be done on the next visit.
*   Preventing equipment loss and unnecessary trips, and making every field visit focused and informed.

### 1.2 Initial Target Users

*   Company Owner / Operations Manager.
*   Field Technicians / Installers.
*   Office Coordinator / Dispatcher (at a later stage).

### 1.3 Technology (V1)

Version 1 (V1) of the system will be a web-based application, responsive and mobile-first for technicians (Android/iOS browsers), and comfortable for desktop use by managers. Equipment will be identified using QR tags (e.g., 001–500), and the location will be based on customer site addresses and optional GPS at the moment of QR scanning. A map view and color-based alerts will help managers quickly see where the equipment is and what requires attention [1].

## 2. Core User Story

As an operations manager who installs and leaves equipment at customer sites for 1–2 weeks, I want to know at any moment where each piece of equipment is, how long it’s been there, and what has been done at each site, so that I can avoid losing equipment, schedule removals on time, and make every visit efficient and purposeful [1].

## 3. Primary User Journeys

### 3.1 "A Day in the Life of a Manager"

**Morning:**

*   The manager logs into the web app (desktop).
*   They see a high-level view:
    *   A list of alerts: equipment past or close to its planned removal date, "risky" sites.
    *   A map of sites with active equipment, colored by urgency.
*   The manager selects several sites that require action and creates Work Orders (installations / inspections / removals), assigning technicians and planned dates.

**During the day:**

*   The manager monitors which Work Orders have been completed.
*   For a problematic site, the manager opens the Site view:
    *   Sees all active equipment at that site, with "removal wheels" (time progress to planned removal).
    *   Reviews the history of past visits (installations, inspections, removals).
    *   Based on this, the manager decides:
        *   To extend the planned removal for some devices.
        *   To schedule an immediate removal for others.

**Evening:**

*   The manager opens the Equipment list:
    *   Filters to "At customer" + "Past removal date" or "Long time at site".
    *   Identifies "stuck" equipment and plans follow-up visits [1].

### 3.2 "A Day in the Life of a Technician"

**Morning:**

*   The technician opens the web app on their phone.
*   They see a list of today’s Work Orders (installations, inspections, removals), with addresses and a "Navigate with Waze" button.
*   They pick the first job and start navigation to the customer site.

**On-site – Installation:**

*   The technician opens the Work Order.
*   They scan the QR tag of the equipment they are installing.
*   The system:
    *   Links this equipment to the customer site.
    *   Sets the installation date to today.
    *   Lets the technician quickly select a planned removal: 1 week / 2 weeks / custom date.
*   The technician completes the checklist (e.g., electrical report, sand, moisture, ring closures).
*   They write what was done (Done) and what should be addressed on the next visit (To Do).

**On-site – Inspection:**

*   For an inspection Work Order, the technician scans all devices found at the site.
*   Each scan confirms the device is still at that site and updates its last scanned timestamp and location.
*   The technician updates the checklist and Done/To Do for that visit, possibly adjusting planned removal dates.

**End of day:**

*   The technician completes the remaining Work Orders.
*   The system updates equipment statuses and locations, and enriches the site and equipment history with all actions performed [1].

## 5. UI & Main Screens

### 5.1 Equipment List & Detail

**Equipment List**

*   Table or card view with: QR Tag, Equipment Type, Status, Site, and a small Removal Progress Wheel icon.
*   **Filters:** At customer / In warehouse / In repair, Past removal date, Close to removal, By site, By equipment type.

**Equipment Detail**

*   Header: Equipment Type + QR Tag.
*   Status & current location: "At: [Site Name]" or "In warehouse".
*   Large Progress Wheel showing time to removal.
*   Dates: Last Installation, Planned Removal, Actual Removal.
*   List of related Work Orders (timeline).
*   **Quick actions:** "Start new Work Order with this equipment," "Mark as removed," "Update condition (fault/wear)." [1]

### 5.2 Sites (Customers)

**Sites List**

*   List with: Site name, Address, Rating, Highlight badge, Number of active equipment, and an indicator of the "worst" removal status at that site.
*   **Filters:** Sites with equipment past removal date, Sites with upcoming removals, High/low rating.

**Site Detail**

*   Full customer information.
*   "Navigate with Waze" button.
*   List of active equipment at this site.
*   History of Work Orders at this site.
*   "Create new Work Order" button.

### 5.3 Work Orders

**Work Orders List**

*   Default view: Today / This week.
*   For each Work Order: Type, Site, Planned/Actual date & time, Technician, Checklist completion status, Status (Open / In progress / Completed).
*   **Filters:** By technician, By type, By status.

**Work Order Detail**

*   Site and technician details.
*   Planned vs actual date/time.
*   Checklist with items and text inputs.
*   "Done" / "To Do" text areas.
*   Equipment section with a "Scan equipment" button.
*   Planned Removal Date selection (1 week / 2 weeks / custom).

### 5.4 Map View

*   Map with markers for sites with active equipment.
*   Marker color/size can reflect the number of devices or urgency.
*   Clicking a marker shows site name, device count by status, and a link to the Site detail.
*   **Filters:** By equipment type, By removal time window, By status.

### 5.5 Alerts / Dashboard

*   List of alert cards grouped by category (e.g., past removal date, approaching removal date).
*   Each card shows equipment details, site, and days past/remaining.
*   **Quick actions:** Open Equipment, Open Site, Create Removal Work Order.
*   V1 alerts are in-app only (no external notifications) [1].

## 6. Key Business Flows

### 6.1 Equipment Lifecycle

1.  **Create equipment:** Manager creates a new equipment entry with QR Tag, Type, and initial status.
2.  **Send for installation:** An Installation Work Order is created.
3.  **Install at customer site:** Technician scans the QR, and the system updates the equipment's location, installation date, and planned removal date.
4.  **Inspections:** Technician scans all devices on-site to confirm their presence and update their status.
5.  **Removal and return:** A Removal Work Order is created. The technician scans the QR upon removal, and the system updates the status to "Warehouse" or "Available" and records the actual removal date.

### 6.2 Checklist & Done/To Do Flow

Each Work Order has a specific checklist. The technician fills it out, records what was "Done," and notes what is "To Do" for the next visit. This information is visible in subsequent Work Orders for continuity.

### 6.3 Alerts & Monitoring Flow

The system periodically recalculates the time to removal for each device and generates alerts for the manager. The manager uses the Alerts screen to take action, such as creating new Work Orders.

## 8. V1 Scope & Future Directions

### In Scope for V1:

*   Web app (no separate native mobile app).
*   QR-based identification of equipment.
*   Full equipment lifecycle management (creation, installation, inspection, removal).
*   Checklists and Done/To Do notes per Work Order.
*   Site and customer data management with Waze navigation integration.
*   Map view of devices by site.
*   In-app alerts for removal timing and long-duration equipment at customer sites.

### Out of Scope for V1 (Planned for later phases):

*   Full role-based access control (RBAC) and complex user hierarchies.
*   Detailed warehouse and vehicle logistics flows.
*   External notifications (email/SMS/WhatsApp).
*   Offline mode functionality.
*   Real-time driver/vehicle tracking.
