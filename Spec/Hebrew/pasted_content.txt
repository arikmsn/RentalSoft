Product Brief – Equipment Tracking & Field Operations System (V1)

1. Context & Vision
This system is designed for companies that install and temporarily leave equipment at customer sites for short periods (typically one to two weeks), similar to rental or temporary deployment.
The main pain points today are: not knowing exactly where each device is, missing removal dates, losing equipment in the field, and lacking a clear history of what was done at each site and what must be done next.

The system’s vision is to become the “brain of the equipment in the field”:

Always know where each tagged device is, at which customer site, and for how long.

See what was done during the last installation/inspection/removal and what needs to happen on the next visit.

Prevent lost equipment and unnecessary trips, and make every field visit focused and informed.

Initial target users:

Company owner / operations manager.

Field technicians / installers.

Office coordinator / dispatcher (later).

Technology-wise, V1 is a web-based application, responsive and mobile-first for technicians (Android/iOS browsers), and comfortable on desktop for managers.
Equipment is identified using QR tags (e.g., 001–500), and location is based on customer site addresses and optional GPS at the moment of QR scanning. A map view and color-based alerts help managers quickly see where equipment is and what requires attention.

2. Core User Story
As an operations manager who installs and leaves equipment at customer sites for 1–2 weeks,
I want to know at any moment where each piece of equipment is, how long it’s been there, and what has been done at each site,
so that I can avoid losing equipment, schedule removals on time, and make every visit efficient and purposeful.

3. Primary User Journeys
3.1 “A Day in the Life of a Manager”
Morning:

The manager logs into the web app (desktop).

They see a high-level view:

A list of alerts: equipment past or close to planned removal date, “risky” sites.

A map of sites with active equipment, colored by urgency.

The manager selects several sites that require action and creates Work Orders (installations / inspections / removals), assigning technicians and planned dates.

During the day:

The manager monitors which Work Orders have been completed.

For a problematic site, the manager opens the Site view:

Sees all active equipment at that site, with “removal wheels” (time progress to planned removal).

Reviews the history of past visits (installations, inspections, removals).

Based on this, the manager decides:

To extend planned removal for some devices.

To schedule an immediate removal for others.

Evening:

The manager opens the Equipment list:

Filters to “At customer” + “Past removal date” or “Long time at site”.

Identifies “stuck” equipment and plans follow-up visits.

3.2 “A Day in the Life of a Technician”
Morning:

The technician opens the web app on their phone.

They see a list of today’s Work Orders (installations, inspections, removals), with addresses and a “Navigate with Waze” button.

They pick the first job and start navigation to the customer site.

On site – Installation:

The technician opens the Work Order.

They scan the QR tag of the equipment they are installing.

The system:

Links this equipment to the customer site.

Sets the installation date to today.

Lets the technician quickly select a planned removal: 1 week / 2 weeks / custom date.

The technician completes the checklist (e.g., electrical report, sand, moisture, ring closures).

They write what was done (Done) and what should be addressed on the next visit (To Do).

On site – Inspection:

For an inspection Work Order, the technician scans all devices found at the site.

Each scan confirms the device is still at that site and updates its last scanned timestamp and location.

The technician updates the checklist and Done/To Do for that visit, possibly adjusting planned removal dates.

End of day:

The technician completes remaining Work Orders.

The system updates equipment statuses and locations, and enriches site and equipment history with all actions performed.

4. Core Modules & Entities
4.1 Equipment
Represents a single physical device with a unique QR tag.

Key fields:

Equipment ID (internal).

QR Tag (e.g., 001–500, unique).

Equipment Type:

Created/managed by the manager.

Technicians can only select existing types (no creation).

Status:

Location status: At customer / In warehouse / In vehicle (future) / In repair / Available.

Condition: OK / Not OK / Wear-out.

Current Location:

Linked Site (customer) if at a customer.

“Warehouse” (no site) if in stock.

“Vehicle X” in future phases.

Dates:

Last Installation Date.

Planned Removal Date (from Work Orders; defaults: 1 week / 2 weeks / custom).

Actual Removal Date (if removed).

Last “move” date (last location change / “drag” event).

Removal Progress Wheel:

Percentage of time elapsed between installation and planned removal.

Color-coded:

Green: plenty of time remaining.

Yellow: medium.

Orange: close to removal.

Red: past planned removal.

Fault / wear history: linked to Activities.

4.2 Site (Customer)
Represents a physical location where equipment is installed.

Key fields:

Site ID.

Name (customer / site).

Address (including city).

Additional details:

Floor.

Apartment.

Contact Person 1 (name, phone).

Contact Person 2 (optional).

Rating (e.g., internal score).

Highlight flag (important or problematic customer).

Coordinates (lat/lng):

Derived from the address (geocoding) or from GPS at first QR scan on-site.

Waze integration:

“Navigate” button opens Waze with this site as the destination.

4.3 Work Order (Job)
Represents any field activity at a site.

Key fields:

WorkOrder ID.

Type: Installation / Inspection / Removal / General.

Planned date & time.

Actual date & time.

Assigned technician (User).

Linked Site.

Linked equipment list:

Equipment used/installed/inspected/removed during this work.

Done / To Do:

Two text fields:

Done – what was actually done during this visit.

To Do – what must be done next time.

Checklist:

Fields like: Electrical report, “SomSom”, sand, moisture check, ring closure, etc.

Manager can define/edit the checklist type; technicians can only use existing checklists.

Planned Removal Date (if relevant):

Choice: 1 week / 2 weeks / custom date.

This also updates the associated equipment’s Planned Removal Date.

4.4 Activity Log
An immutable history of actions on equipment and sites.

Key fields:

Activity ID.

Timestamp (when the action occurred).

Action type, e.g.:

Equipment status change (OK / Not OK / wear).

Location change (warehouse → customer, customer → warehouse, etc.).

Work Order created / completed.

Checklist updates.

Done / To Do updates.

Planned removal date changes.

Links:

Equipment ID (if relevant).

WorkOrder ID.

Site ID.

Performed by (User).

Location of action (if QR was scanned):

GPS coordinates + user name.

Editing policy:

V1: all users can edit past dates/values (business-level), but changes are still logged.

Later: only managers can edit history.

4.5 Users
Basic identity for now (no strict RBAC yet).

Key fields:

User ID.

Full name.

Phone.

Role label: Manager / Technician / Office.

Active / inactive.

A proper role-based permission model will be added in a later phase.

Sites List

Address.

Rating.

Number of active equipment at site.

A small indicator of “worst” removal status at that site (e.g., if any device is in red).

Sites with equipment past removal date.

Full customer information.

“Navigate with Waze” button.

Equipment Type, QR, Progress Wheel.

5.3 Work Orders
Work Orders List

Default view: Today / This week.

For each Work Order:

Type (icon).

Site.

Planned date & time (and actual if completed).

Technician.

Checklist completion status (e.g., 3/5 items done).

Status: Open / In progress / Completed.

Filters:

By technician.

By type.

By status.

Work Order Detail

Site and technician details.

Planned vs actual date/time.

Checklist:

Items like electrical report, “SomSom”, sand, moisture, ring closure – checkboxes and optional text inputs.

Done / To Do text areas with timestamps.

Equipment section:

List of linked equipment.

“Scan equipment” button:

Opens camera.

Scanning QR adds the equipment to this Work Order and updates its location to this site.

Planned Removal Date (if type requires):

5.4 Map View
Map with markers for sites that currently have active equipment.

Marker color/size can reflect:

Number of devices at the site.

Highest urgency among devices (e.g., if any device is red, the site marker is red).

Clicking a marker shows:

Site name.

Count of devices by removal status (e.g., 3 green, 1 orange, 2 red).

Link to Site detail.

Filters:

By equipment type.

By removal time window:

Must be removed this week.

Past removal date.

By status (OK / Not OK / wear).

5.5 Alerts / Dashboard
List of alert cards grouped by category:

Devices past planned removal date.

Devices close to planned removal (orange/red).

Devices staying at a customer longer than a configured threshold.

Each alert card shows:

Equipment Type, QR Tag.

Site.

Days past/remaining.

Quick actions:

Open Equipment.

Open Site.

Create Removal Work Order.

V1: alerts are shown only inside the web app (no email/SMS/WhatsApp yet).

6. Key Business Flows
6.1 Equipment Lifecycle
Create equipment

Manager creates a new Equipment entry.

Sets QR Tag, Equipment Type, initial status (In warehouse).

Send equipment for installation

Manager/office creates an Installation Work Order.

Technician physically takes the device (formal warehouse/vehicle flows will come later).

Install at customer site

Technician scans the QR at the customer site.

System:

Sets location to the Site.

Sets installation date to today.

Prompts for planned removal (1 week / 2 weeks / custom date).

Inspections

Each inspection is a separate Work Order.

Technician scans all devices at the site:

Confirms presence at this site.

Updates last scan timestamp and position.

Technician may adjust planned removal dates and statuses.

Removal and return

Removal Work Order is created.

On site, technician scans the QR when taking the device back.

System:

Changes location to “Warehouse” / “Available”.

Sets actual removal date.

Resets the removal progress wheel.

6.2 Checklist & Done/To Do Flow
Each Work Order contains a checklist specific to its type.

During the visit, the technician:

Checks relevant items.

Writes what was done (Done).

Writes what must be done next time (To Do).

On the next visit (new Work Order), the technician sees the previous To Do and can seamlessly continue.

6.3 Alerts & Monitoring Flow
Periodically or on-demand, the system recalculates for each device:

Time elapsed between installation and planned removal.

Whether the device is past, close to, or far from removal date.

It compiles an alerts list for:

Devices with removal date in the past.

Devices approaching removal date.

Devices at customer sites longer than a threshold.

The manager uses the Alerts screen to:

Open Equipment / Site details.

Create new Work Orders (usually removals or inspections).

7. Architecture Overview (High-Level)
7.1 Layers
Presentation Layer (Web Client)

Responsive web application:

Desktop UX optimized for managers and office users.

Mobile-first UX for technicians (phone browsers, camera access for QR).

Includes views for Equipment, Sites, Work Orders, Map, Alerts.

Integrates with:

Device camera (for QR scanning via browser APIs).

Waze (via URL deep-link from site address/coordinates).

Application Layer (Backend API)

REST or GraphQL API powering all UI operations.

Core services:

Inventory Service: equipment data, statuses, locations, removal progress.

Work Orders Service: job lifecycle and association with equipment/sites.

Sites/CRM Service: customer/site data and metadata.

Activity & Audit Service: action logging and history.

Alerts & Scheduler Service: periodic evaluation of removal dates and conditions.

Location & Map Service: managing coordinates and data aggregation for the map.

Data Layer (Database)

Relational database (e.g., PostgreSQL).

Core tables: Equipment, Sites, WorkOrders, Activities, Users, Alerts, Checklists.

Indexed for common queries: by QR Tag, Site, status, and date ranges.

Integration Layer

Map provider (Google Maps / OpenStreetMap or similar).

Optional geocoding service for address → coordinates.

Future phase: integration with email/SMS/WhatsApp providers for external notifications.

8. V1 Scope & Future Directions
In scope for V1:

Web app (no separate native mobile app).

QR-based identification of equipment.

Equipment lifecycle (creation, installation, inspection, removal).

Checklists, Done/To Do per Work Order.

Sites & customer data with Waze navigation.

Map view of devices by site.

In-app alerts for removal timing and long-duration equipment at customers.

Out of scope for V1 (planned for later phases):

Full role-based permissions and complex hierarchy (Manager / Technician / Office with restricted access).

Detailed warehouse/vehicle logistics flows.

External notifications (email/SMS/WhatsApp).

Offline mode.

Realtime driver/vehicle tracking.
